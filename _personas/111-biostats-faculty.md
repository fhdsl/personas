title: Biostatistics Faculty Member
---
## Job Title(s)
Associate Professor of Biostatistics and Machine Learning

## Major Responsibilities: 
Teaching and conducting research in the fields of biostatistics and machine learning, with a focus on healthcare applications.

## Nature of Interactions with Data and Tools: 
Interacts with large datasets from various sources, including electronic health records (EHRs), medical claims data, and other healthcare databases. 


## Data Domain and Types of Interest: 
Interested in accessing raw clinical EHR data to build machine learning models that predict health outcomes such as disease progression and treatment response.

## Software Expertise, Attitude and Use: 
- Highly proficient in programming languages and statistical analysis software. 
- Has a deep understanding of machine learning algorithms and their applications in healthcare. 
- Willing to learn new approaches to accessing data.  

Uses programming languages such as:
- R  
- Python 
- SAS

## Needs and Wants: 
Needs access to large, high-quality datasets to build accurate machine learning models. Wants to ensure that data is de-identified and compliant with regulations such as HIPAA.

## Key Challenges: 
May encounter challenges with obtaining access to raw clinical EHR data due to privacy concerns and data access restrictions. Often there is no support for them to navigate this challenge.

Needs to ensure that machine learning models are accurate, unbiased, and interpretable. 

Additionally, may need to work with healthcare professionals to understand clinical context and validate model performance in real-world settings.

## Orientation and Tolerance to Change
Open to new methodologies, tools, and practices, especially as they pertain to machine learning and biostatistics. The nature of the field requires constant learning and adaptation.

## Work, Collaboration and Communication Patterns
Frequently collaborates with other researchers, statisticians, healthcare professionals, and students. More immediate communication occurs through meetings, emails, instant messaging (Teams/Slack). Longer term communication occurs via research papers, conferences, perhaps a professional blog.

## Funding and Reporting Model
Primarily supported through research grants, some institutional support, perhaps teaching responsibilities but this is rarely the case at Fred Hutch. Required to report progress annually to the department chair and during the tenure review process.

## Metrics of Success and Motivations
Success is measured by the impact of their research (citations, awards), the performance of their students, the attainment of research funding, and contributions to the field of biostatistics and machine learning. They are motivated by the potential to improve healthcare outcomes through their research.

## Professional Development Goals
The desire to stay current with the rapidly evolving field of machine learning, continue to publish high-impact research, and seek opportunities for collaboration with other researchers and clinicians. They may also aspire to gain recognition as a leader in their field through writing, speaking, interacting with the media, and influencing policy.
