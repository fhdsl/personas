# Translational Research Analytics Analyst

## Job Title
Translational Research Analytics Manager
## Major Responsibilities
Leads a group of analysts primarily supporting data access, facilitation and support for translational research using FHCC clinical data. 
•	Develops and manage a team primarily responsible for facilitating research use of FHCC clinical data assets, creating internal processes and data resources to create an effective team that defines success as success of the researchers they work with.
•	Works with researchers, data governance staff, and IT staff to develop and socialize processes (both automated and human) that improve data accessibility while protecting FHCC patients by respecting patient consent, privacy and agency.
•	Mentors and trains analysts and research staff in best practices of documentation of analyses, appropriate use of data, and data science skills. 
•	Provides direct support for facilitated data request fulfillment as well as supporting self-service data access.

## Approach and Tolerance to Change
 Endeavors to remain knowledgeable of the ever-changing landscape regarding data availability and governance. From content to specific technical issues with extraction via joins and filtering, to access rights and documenting data provenance.
## Broad Needs and Wants
Would benefit from being made aware of any planned data source/content changes as well as IT support resources. (example- SCCA IT integration following the merger). Also a clear understanding of the data lineage would be very helpful.
## Broad Challenges
It is difficult to measure the completeness (or limitations) of any given data result. Treatment medications is a good example of this challenge. There’s currently a variety of source tables with different formats. Add to that the category of 'research' treatments and a patient’s potential for being treated at an ‘outside’ location and of course there’s adherence considerations. In contrast the completeness of lab values can be relatively clearly described. 
It would be reassuring to a researcher/consumer to be able to say the results are both complete and exhaustive.
